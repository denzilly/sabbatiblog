01 · May 23–24

Nikko日光 · 2 nights

Tochigi Prefecture · Train from Tokyo

The perfect opening act — ornate UNESCO shrines deep in the mountains, then a full day in the highlands. Fly into Tokyo, train straight here.

  • Tōshō-gū Shrine complex — lavish Edo-period mausoleum
  • Senjogahara Marshlands — easy boardwalk hike
  • Mt. Nantai option for a serious climb (6 hrs)
  • Soak in a mountain onsen after the hike

02 · May 25–27

Sendai & Matsushima松島 · 3 nights

Miyagi Prefecture · Shinkansen north

One of Japan's three great views — 260 pine-covered islands across a calm bay. Sendai is a real northern city with a great food scene.

  • Matsushima boat cruise between the islands
  • Walk the coastal temple trail on Oshima island
  • Gyutan (beef tongue) dinner in Sendai
  • Zuihō-den mausoleum & Date Masamune history
Matsushima Bay
Godai-dō Temple

03 · May 28–29

Sanriku Coast三陸 · 2 nights

Iwate Prefecture · Local train east

The rugged Pacific coastline of Tohoku — rebuilt after 2011, and all the more moving for it. Sea cliffs, fishing villages, and one of Japan's most beautiful beaches.

  • Jodogahama Beach — white volcanic rocks, electric-blue water
  • Blue cave boat tour from the beach
  • Michinoku Coastal Trail — hike a scenic section
  • Sanriku Railway ride through fishing villages
Jodogahama Beach
Michinoku Trail
Goishi Coast
04 · May 30–Jun 1

Aomori青森 · 3 nights

Aomori Prefecture · Shinkansen north

The wild tip of Honshu. Three days for one of Japan's most spectacular gorge walks, a volcanic summit, and a world-class festival museum.

  • Oirase Gorge — 14km riverside walk, waterfalls, old-growth forest
  • Mt. Iwaki hike — the 'Fuji of Tsugaru' (6 hrs return)
  • Hirosaki Castle & park
  • WA·RASSE Nebuta Museum — giant illuminated festival floats
Oirase Gorge

05 · Jun 2–3

Hakodate函館 · 2 nights

Hokkaido · Ferry from Aomori

A port city unlike anywhere else in Japan — colonial churches, a harbour of old warehouses, and what might be the country's most famous night view.

  • Mt. Hakodate night panorama by ropeway
  • Hakodate Morning Market — fresh uni, squid, crab
  • Motomachi colonial quarter — consulates, churches
  • Goryokaku star fort & tower
Night Panorama

06 · Jun 4–6

Daisetsuzan大雪山 · 3 nights

Central Hokkaido · Train + bus

The wild heart of Hokkaido — Japan's largest national park. Volcanic peaks, basalt gorges, steaming onsen, and early alpine flowers. The trip's hiking climax.

  • Mt. Asahidake summit attempt — Hokkaido's highest peak (2,291m)
  • Asahidake Ropeway to the volcanic vent fields
  • Sounkyo Gorge trail — waterfalls, basalt cliffs
  • Mountain onsen lodge recovery day
Mt. Asahidake
Sounkyo Gorge

07 · Jun 7–9

Tokyo東京 · 3 nights · departure

Shinkansen back south

The trip ends where Japan always surprises — the city. No obligations, no schedule. Good food, a neighbourhood you haven't explored, and a slow departure.

  • Decompress — no agenda required
  • Revisit a favourite neighbourhood or find a new one
  • Final ramen, sushi, izakaya crawl
  • Easy airport transfer from anywhere in the city
